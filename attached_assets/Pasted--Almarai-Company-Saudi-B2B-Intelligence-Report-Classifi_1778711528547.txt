## 1. Overview

**Almarai Company** (Arabic: المراعي) is the largest vertically integrated dairy and food company in the Middle East and one of the most recognized consumer brands in the Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC). The company operates across the entire food value chain — from raw agriculture and animal husbandry through to processing, packaging, and last-mile retail distribution — giving it a structural competitive advantage that few regional peers can replicate.

Almarai was originally established as a partnership between Irish agribusiness firm **Masstock** and the **Al Saud Al Kabeer** family. Over three decades it has grown into a multi-category food conglomerate with the largest private dairy herd in the world, producing over one billion units of product annually. Its distribution network — comprising thousands of refrigerated routes across the GCC — is widely regarded as a strategic moat.

## 6. Operations & Industry

### 6.1 Business Segments

Almarai operates across six primary business divisions:

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| **Dairy & Juice** | Core segment; fresh milk, long-life milk, laban, yogurt, cream, fruit juices |
| **Bakery** | Western Bakeries and L'usine brands; bread, pastries, cakes |
| **Poultry** | Integrated broiler operations under the **Alyoum** brand |
| **Infant Nutrition** | Joint venture with **Mead Johnson** (now Reckitt); formula products |
| **Arable / Agriculture** | Owns large-scale farms in Argentina (Al Dahra) for feed crop cultivation |
| **International** | Operations in Jordan (International Dairy & Juice — IDJ JV with PepsiCo) and Egypt |

### 6.2 Vertical Integration Model

Almarai's competitive differentiation is anchored in **full vertical integration**: it owns and operates its own farms (including the world's largest single dairy herd, historically located in the Thumamah area near Riyadh, though farms span multiple regions), feed production, processing plants, packaging, cold-chain logistics, and a proprietary route-to-market distribution network. This model insulates margins relative to peers dependent on external supply chains.

### 6.3 Market Position

- **Dairy:** Dominant market leader in Saudi Arabia and GCC with estimated market shares exceeding 40% in fresh dairy categories.
- **Bakery:** Market leader in KSA packaged bread and pastry segment.
- **Poultry:** Significant player in the Saudi fresh poultry market.
- **Distribution:** One of the largest refrigerated distribution networks in the Middle East.
